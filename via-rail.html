What it is
An onboard Wi-Fi landing page that manages customer expectations by presenting relevant information in realtime.
What I did
UI / UX Designer + Frontend Engineer + Data Scientist in collaboration with other business units.
What I used
Bootstrap + Mapbox light-weight landing page with custom machine learning.

Soothing passengers using realtime data

VIA Rail has two big problems it can't control. Their passenger trains have to give the right of way to freight trains, and the cellular network their onboard Wi-Fi system connects to is limited.

The math isn't difficult:

Delays + Unreliable Wi-Fi = Unhappy Customers

I started by talking to passengers in stations and on trains, both as an official representative of VIA and undercover. We learned that passengers were frustrated by the delays and Wi-Fi, but they were angry about not knowing why and for how long.

As a passenger, these questions often generate anxiety:

  • Where am I currently?
  • When will I get to my destination?
  • Why isn't my Instagram loading?
  • Does the Wi-Fi work at all?
  • Will my call get dropped?
How do you create control that doesn't exist?

VIA Rail had already developed a simple HTML webpage that used realtime train data to display the a given train's schedule and location in realtime. Unusually, the tool wasn't advertised to passengers at all and was considered one of VIA's "best kept secrets".

The application was an excellent foundation, but it needed some work. The map looks nice, but it feels like I'm floating in the void. Where are the stations? Where did I start? Where am I going?

As I prototyped the new app, I frequently boarded train to get passenger opinions, allowing for uniquely continuous design validation.

The enhancement required aggregating millions of raw Wi-Fi diagnostic data points into GeoJSON fragments. Using Python, the GeoJSON data could then be divided into segments based on average expected Wi-Fi speed. With improved location context using the map, and information about the current Wi-Fi status, the application was ready to become the new official Wi-Fi landing page.
