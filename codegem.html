Engineering culture, one pull request at a time

When my friends and I founded CodeGem two years ago, we wanted to build a framework to help engineers give and recieve better feedback.

After interviewing engineers and managers at Shopify and Loblaw Digital, we quickly learned that there was a huge amount of missing data about how engineering teams actually work together.

Understanding the content and context of engineering communication

A lot can be learned by listening. Using natural language processing, we can piece together how developers collaborate, tie communication to output, and present the big picture.

Since engineers need as much time as they can get, CodeGem injects HTML straight into GitHub so that they can interact with helpful features without extra clicks.

Grow as a team, empowered by data

A healthy engineering culture promotes mentorship and personal growth. CodeGem supports developers by allowing them to set quantative goals, and then seek feedback from their team to measure progress.

By analyzing the feedback over time, teams can start to measure their strengths, areas to improve, and blind spots. Combine this data with velocity and coding output metrics, and it is possible to create a comprehensive picture of the team's productivity and contribution to success, backed by data.
